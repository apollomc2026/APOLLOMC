INCIDENT REPORT

Incident Date: April 20, 2026
Time: 14:27
Location: Conference Room B, 2nd floor, Summit Place
Reported by: Jordan Kim, Facilities Manager

Incident Summary

On April 20, 2026, at 14:27, a fire sprinkler system discharged without cause in Conference Room B, releasing approximately 18 gallons of water before being shut off at 14:35. Six operations team members were conducting a meeting at the time of the incident. No injuries occurred.

Detailed Description

At 14:27, the overhead fire sprinkler in Conference Room B began discharging water without any preceding fire, smoke, or alarm activation. The operations team meeting was in progress with six attendees present. Water discharge continued for eight minutes until the building engineer shut off the riser valve at 14:35. The discharge volume is estimated at 18 gallons based on the extent of water damage and pooling observed.

Immediate Response

At 14:29, all occupants were evacuated from Conference Room B. Building engineer was contacted at 14:30 and responded to shut off the water supply riser at 14:35. Water vacuum equipment was deployed by 14:50 to extract standing water from the conference room floor. The affected meeting was immediately relocated to an alternate conference room to continue operations.

Witnesses and Statements

Eight witnesses were present: all six meeting attendees from the operations team, plus two staff members from adjacent offices who responded when the incident alarm sounded. All witnesses confirm no fire, smoke, unusual heat, or other environmental factors preceded the sprinkler activation.

Damage Assessment

Conference Room B sustained water damage requiring immediate remediation. Carpet throughout the room was saturated and requires replacement. Four conference room chairs sustained water damage. One laptop computer belonging to a meeting attendee was water-damaged and is pending full assessment. Ceiling tiles directly below the sprinkler head require replacement due to water staining and structural compromise.

Photos

No photos were provided with this report.

Root Cause Analysis

Preliminary hypothesis indicates thermal sensor malfunction as the likely cause of the unwarranted sprinkler activation. This assessment is pending confirmation through inspection by the building's contracted fire-system vendor, which has been scheduled.

Recommendations

The following actions are recommended to prevent recurrence and address current damage:

  1. Schedule comprehensive inspection of all thermal sensors on the 2nd floor by the building fire-system vendor
  2. Replace affected ceiling tiles and carpet in Conference Room B
  3. Document all property losses and submit insurance claim for damaged equipment and materials
