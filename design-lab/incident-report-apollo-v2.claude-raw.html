INCIDENT REPORT

Incident Date: April 20, 2026
Time: 14:27
Location: Conference Room B, 2nd floor, Summit Place
Reporter: Jordan Kim, Facilities Manager

Incident Summary

On April 20, 2026, at 14:27, an overhead fire sprinkler discharged without warning in Conference Room B on the second floor of Summit Place. Six operations team members were conducting a meeting when approximately 18 gallons of water released from the sprinkler system. The discharge occurred without any preceding fire, smoke, or heat source. Building occupants were evacuated and the water supply was shut off by 14:35.

Detailed Description

At 14:27, water began discharging from the overhead sprinkler head located in the center of Conference Room B. The six operations team members attending the meeting immediately noticed the water discharge. No fire, smoke, heat, or other triggering conditions were present in the room. The sprinkler system activation appeared to be spontaneous. Water discharged continuously at full pressure until the building engineer shut off the riser valve at 14:35, resulting in an estimated 18 gallons of water released into the room. The conference room's fire alarm system activated concurrently with the sprinkler discharge.

Immediate Response

Room occupants were evacuated at 14:29, two minutes after the initial discharge. The building engineer was contacted at 14:30 and arrived on scene by 14:33. The water supply riser was shut off at 14:35, stopping further discharge. Water vacuum equipment was deployed by 14:50 to begin water extraction from the affected area. The scheduled meeting was relocated to an alternate conference room. Conference Room B was marked as unusable pending restoration.

Witnesses and Statements

Eight witnesses were present during or immediately after the incident. All six operations team meeting attendees observed the initial sprinkler discharge. Two staff members from adjacent offices responded to the fire alarm and witnessed the water damage and evacuation procedures. Witness contact information has been documented separately for potential follow-up.

Damage Assessment

The incident resulted in property damage but no personal injuries. Conference room carpet became fully saturated with water. Four office chairs sustained water damage and require assessment for replacement. One laptop computer was exposed to water and is pending technical assessment to determine repair or replacement needs. Six ceiling tiles directly below the sprinkler head were damaged and require replacement. The conference room remains unusable pending carpet replacement and full restoration.

Photos

No photographic documentation was provided for this incident.

Root Cause Analysis

Preliminary assessment suggests thermal sensor malfunction as the probable cause of the false activation. The sprinkler head thermal sensor may have triggered incorrectly without adequate heat source. This hypothesis requires verification through professional inspection by the building's fire system vendor. No other contributing factors have been identified at this time.

Recommendations

The following actions are recommended to prevent recurrence and complete incident resolution:

  1. Schedule immediate inspection by building fire system vendor to examine all thermal sensors on the second floor
  2. Replace damaged ceiling tiles and carpet in Conference Room B
  3. Document all property losses for insurance claim submission
  4. Consider implementing quarterly fire system maintenance inspections to identify potential sensor malfunctions
