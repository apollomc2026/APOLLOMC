# Legal Modern — Style Specification
**Version:** 1
**Industry:** Legal

## Typography
- Heading font: Garamond Premier Pro
- Body font: Garamond
- Heading sizes: H1 26pt semibold, H2 20pt semibold, H3 16pt semibold, H4 13pt bold
- Body size: 11.5pt
- Line height: 1.45
- Footnote size: 9.5pt
- Caption size: 9pt italic

## Color palette
- Primary: #3D5A80 — use for headings, horizontal rules, and sidebar accents
- Secondary: #98C1D9 — use for highlight boxes, callout backgrounds, and table headers
- Accent: #EE6C4D — use only for urgent flags or action-required indicators
- Body text: #2E2E2E
- Background: #FFFFFF
- Light background blocks: #F7F9FC
- Table header background: #3D5A80 with white text
- Table borders: #D1D9E6

## Layout rules
- Page size: 8.5in x 11in (US Letter)
- Margins: 0.9in top, 0.9in bottom, 1in left, 1in right
- Section spacing: 20pt before H1, 16pt before H2, 10pt before H3
- Paragraph spacing: 8pt after each paragraph
- Column structure: single column for body; two-column layout permitted for executive summary sidebars
- Page numbers: bottom right, 9pt, simple numeral
- Header: thin #3D5A80 rule across top with document title left-aligned, date right-aligned, 8.5pt
- Footer: thin #D1D9E6 rule with confidentiality note centered, 8pt

## Formatting conventions
- Section numbering: decimal (1.0, 1.1, 1.2) rather than Roman numerals
- Key findings presented in light blue (#F7F9FC) callout boxes with left border accent
- Defined terms in bold on first use, with a definitions appendix for complex documents
- Legal citations follow Bluebook but with minimal formatting (no full italics on case names — use regular weight)
- Pull quotes for critical holdings: indented, 13pt, #3D5A80 left border
- Bullet lists preferred over numbered lists for non-sequential items
- Tables with rounded header styling and generous cell padding (8pt)
- Signature blocks: clean horizontal rule, left-aligned

## Tone and voice
- Professional but accessible — avoid archaic legal phrasing where modern equivalents exist
- Prefer active voice: "The court held" over "It was held by the court"
- Contractions acceptable in client-facing memos (not in filings)
- Explain legal concepts with brief parenthetical context for non-lawyer stakeholders
- Maintain analytical rigor while prioritizing readability
- Use "we" when representing the firm's position in advisory memos
- Direct recommendations encouraged: "We recommend" rather than "It may be advisable"

## What this style MUST produce
- Clean, contemporary legal documents that balance professionalism with readability
- Decimal section numbering throughout (1.0, 1.1, 2.0)
- At least one callout box per major section highlighting key findings
- Executive summary on page 1 for documents exceeding 6 pages
- Consistent header and footer treatment on every page
- Definitions appendix if more than 5 defined terms are used
- Page count: per deliverable type specification
- All sections in the order specified by the deliverable module
