# Legal Classic — Style Specification
**Version:** 1
**Industry:** Legal

## Typography
- Heading font: Times New Roman
- Body font: Times New Roman
- Heading sizes: H1 28pt bold, H2 22pt bold, H3 18pt bold, H4 14pt bold italic
- Body size: 12pt
- Line height: 1.5 (double-spaced for court filings, 1.5 for memos)
- Footnote size: 10pt
- Block quote indent: 0.5in from both margins, italic

## Color palette
- Primary: #1B2A4A — use for headings, section dividers, and key legal citations
- Secondary: #8B0000 — use sparingly for critical warnings, deadline callouts, or action-required flags
- Body text: #1A1A1A
- Background: #FFFFFF
- Table header background: #1B2A4A with white text
- Table alternating rows: #F5F5F0

## Layout rules
- Page size: 8.5in x 11in (US Letter)
- Margins: 1in top, 1in bottom, 1.25in left, 1in right
- Section spacing: 24pt before H1, 18pt before H2, 12pt before H3
- Paragraph spacing: 6pt after each paragraph
- Column structure: single column throughout
- Page numbers: bottom center, 10pt, format "Page X of Y"
- Header: firm/client name right-aligned, 9pt italic, starting page 2
- Footer: confidentiality notice centered, 8pt, light gray

## Formatting conventions
- Legal citations in Bluebook format (italicized case names)
- Numbered paragraphs for findings, conclusions, and contract clauses
- Roman numeral section numbering (I, II, III) for top-level sections
- Alphabetical sub-numbering (a, b, c) for subsections
- All defined terms in bold on first use
- Cross-references hyperlinked where format supports it
- Tables of contents for documents exceeding 5 pages
- Signature blocks: right-aligned, 3in from left margin, with underline rule
- Exhibits labeled alphabetically (Exhibit A, Exhibit B)

## Tone and voice
- Formal, precise, and authoritative
- Use passive voice where convention demands (e.g., "It is hereby ordered")
- Avoid contractions entirely
- Prefer established legal phrasing over plain-language alternatives
- Maintain objective analytical tone in memos; persuasive but measured in briefs
- Never use first person; use "Counsel," "the Firm," or "the undersigned"
- Hedging language where appropriate: "it appears," "the weight of authority suggests"

## What this style MUST produce
- Professional legal documents indistinguishable from top-tier AmLaw firm output
- Proper Bluebook citation formatting throughout
- Consistent Roman numeral and alphabetical numbering hierarchy
- Confidentiality footer on every page
- Table of contents for documents over 5 pages
- Signature block where document type requires it
- Page count: per deliverable type specification (typically 4-20 pages)
- All sections in the order specified by the deliverable module
